My Fable

"Do you know that the average human eye blinks 20, 000 times every day?" what! I said. I remember it very well. I was boarding the USIU-Africa university Bus. It happened that I was talking to my friend Jimmy, who I had acquainted with a few minutes ago. We were sitting on the same column and row. On the bus aisle, there was a pile of books. Nothing special about them but one thing interested me and kept me engrossed---the topics: Smelling CODE, SOLID Principles, How to Refactor code, Simplified Algorithms, and Data structures, Clean Architecture by Uncle Bob, Object Oriented and Functional Programming, Onion Architecture, Agile Development, and more amazing topics like Program in Java in 21 days. The list is endless. I constantly stared at them until everyone could notice it. "What...?" said Jimmy. "I mean what...?" I said. "You are occupied bro!" said Jimmy while trying to get a view of the corridor.

I did not know Jimmy for long but I can describe Jimmy in three adjectives: amiable, easygoing, and likable. He did not seem like the other guy on the window seat. He looked undisturbed and informed person. I could guess his interest in reading. Regardless of our alternate laughs, he was reading an eye-attracting book titled: 'How to Jump through a Window?' He did not turn left or right (looking through the window). He kept smiling minute after minute. Was he a student? What unit is that? Finally, I guessed he was an "IT GUY". I had heard about how they ("IT GUYS") were so...so...so committed to creating uns and ins products ---that is invisible products and untouchable products. However, it was more likely to suppose he was doing a Bachelor of Science in Theft.

Did not still understand how code could smell? or in other words, how the system of rules could smell or even worse refactored or created in objects, among other topics. "I need to read this," I was just thinking over. From that moment I kept reminding myself I needed to explore the topics. I finally learned from my friend Jimmy that they were more IT books. The IT extremely motivated me more than the word Chinese. I heard it the first time when I was little. I once knew Chinese people as extra talented Kenyan people but sneeze a lot. Thanks, Jimmy, I know better today.

Finally, the bus pulled over and I went out.

"Bye Jimmy"

"Bye John. By the way, make your Google and Youtube your best friend" said Jimmy, aloud. Not caring about the surrounding.

I lastly broached the subject with my parents. I told them how people are spending 4-year college degree learning how to make code not smell and even testing it. He was so intrigued by the subject. I even added that people are doing masters for the same subject. This subject (making code not smell and testing it) and spending more time on it did not bother them. What disturbed them is that I had the same interest in the subject. Especially, my close mum, I recall how she conversed decisively and in a lowered voice, "Please son do not waste your time". I knew the urge by just looking at her. Nevertheless, this did not help. But I was glad I had my friends --Google and Youtube. This friend amazingly never tire of question (searches).

Eventually, I had a resourceful idea. I perceived it from Jimmy; 20, 000 eye blinks per day. I thought it would be a good idea to create software that can convert those blicks into a shilling per blick. Sadly, someone stole the idea. I was not sad about the incident. I am actually waiting for the project to come to reality. I know you are also waiting for it.

To end it all, I know code is never written but always rewritten.
